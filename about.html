ABOUT MATH-UP

Started by students who
needed this too.

Math-Up Uganda began with a simple observation: good A-Level Math help shouldn't depend on which school you're at or what you can afford.

Our story

From one classroom problem to a Uganda-wide effort.

Math-Up Uganda was co-founded by Jonathan Mukalazi Benoni, Elly Harvey Mukembo, and Kiti Brian — three people who had all gone through the A-Level syllabus themselves and remembered exactly where it gets hard: the topics textbooks rush through, the past papers nobody walks you through properly, the gap between "I understand the theory" and "I can actually solve this in an exam."

What started as informal help between students turned into something more structured: free live seminars held on Zoom, open to any A-Level student in Uganda, paired with a YouTube channel so the same explanations stay available long after a seminar ends.

Beyond the seminars, the team also offers holiday tutoring in mathematics and physics — and can take on students in other subjects depending on need, with in-person sessions available for those who prefer them.

At a glance
  • Focus: A-Level mathematics and physics, with tutoring available in other subjects.
  • Seminars: free, live, and held on Zoom — open to any A-Level student in Uganda.
  • Tutoring: primarily holiday tutoring in maths and physics; in-person sessions available depending on the client's location and needs.
  • Team: founded by Jonathan Mukalazi Benoni, Elly Harvey Mukembo, and Kiti Brian.
  • Cost: seminars are free; reach out about tutoring arrangements.
What we believe

A few things we don't compromise on.

Exam-relevant, always

Every seminar and video ties back to what's actually examinable at A-Level — not tangents, not filler.

Free stays free

The core seminars and YouTube lessons are open to any student. Access to good teaching shouldn't be the bottleneck.

Taught by people, not scripts

Every session is led by someone who's actually worked through the same problems — recently enough to remember where they got stuck too.
